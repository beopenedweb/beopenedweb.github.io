Seeing is Believing

Visiting Children at OSHI helps one to understand their problems and how they are being helped through curriculum, training, practice and assistive devices. It is a journey of human excellence, as the Children will be helped not only by one's concern and support but also by enlarging their scope to interact with normal people.

Visiting Ephphatha is not a social trip for a monetary contribution. It is a pilgrimage to help segregated children from the society and periodic visits will go in a long way to help the children and build their confidence. The most important benefit is that they will know that they can interact with all people

Share your Expertise

Ephphatha opens its doors to people from all over the globe, be it doctors, lawyers, teachers or any field - rest assured your presence will make an impact. Participate as volunteers, medical assistants, etc and interact with students, sharing your knowledge and rich experience - bridging diversity to make students more confident.



Interaction with Children

Communicating to children with hearing impairment can be challenging, yet these children are both sensitive and receptive to our every move. That is why we have created a set of guidelines that will help you to effectively interact with them



Interest

By carefully choosing topics of interest for a chat, it would help to motivate the child in case they are self-conscious about engaging in conversation.



Teaching

While teaching these kids, patience is the key. Slow down if necessary so that they can pronounce each syllable as precisely as possible



Time

Giving them time to finish a sentence without interruption, would give them confidence to take their time and articulate themselves.



Importance

By repeating what one heard from them, will show them the importance of knowing if they were understood correctly.



Communication

While communicating with children, you may not be able to grasp certain facets of what they are trying to convey. Try not to pretend to understand, it would give them a false sense of belief that they are actually saying it right.



Diversity

As visitors, volunteers and trainers, we encourage your wealth of expertise and diversity to enable these kids to learn and expand, do examine their potential and correct them when they are wrong.
