OSHI - Oral Higher Secondary School for the Hearing Impaired

Hands-on learning environment specifically designed for the hard-of hearing

Listen, Hear & Speak – Scripting hope for a brighter future

OSHI (Oral Higher Secondary School for the Hearing Impaired) is Ephphatha’s educational wing that has catered to a large number of students throughout the country for the past over 35 years. Our expertise and educational module comprises of best practices consistent to global standards coupled by our own tools and techniques to develop language in hearing impaired children, making us the most innovation specialist school in the country.

Complex methodology made easy:

At OSHI we adopt the Aural - Oral methodology by which a child is taught to rely on words more than signs to communicate with others. This is a process that is specially developed, difficult to impart, yet seamlessly communicated to students. Our dedicated staff works shoulder-to shoulder with students, ensuring they grasp every topic they learn intuitively, helping them push forward in their academics - taking bold strides towards progress.

Indigenous Assistance:

The core group of students at OSHI has Tamil as their mother tongue. Tamil as a phonetics based language is subject to various levels of expressions in sound. To make it easy for our students, we have modified the phonological order to suit their learning ability.

Customized Tools:

We aim to enrich the learning experience by following an advanced framework that combines global best practices with our own customized, never-before used methodologies to simplify the language and speech training process for the children.

Unlike most hearing impaired schools we break the walls of conventional teaching methodologies and identify pain-points to build a better academic program. We have designed and developed learning aids such as flash cards, audio visual aids, classification charts and picture cards to aid in the learning process of the students

Result Oriented Approach:

The consistent results in Government exams, Academic achievements, speech & language evaluations, periodic assessment of articulation abilities, identifying the receptive and expressive language capability of the student using standardized tools, validate the success of our methodology and assures a bright future for the student.

OSHI Objectives:

  • toll Develop an Individual Education Program (IEP) that includes considerations for students’ access to language, mode of communication at home and at school.
  • toll Language development and fluency abilities, communication partners, school placement and other environmental impacts on language and communication needs.
  • toll All students have the opportunity to fully participate in public education experiences which is a critical aspect of communicating with others.
  • toll Create opportunities for direct communication with peers in the child's language and communication mode with the availability of deaf peers and adult role models to complement the child's own communication mode.
  • toll Create Opportunities for direct communication in the student’s preferred language and communication mode for their academics, with communication accessibility for academic instruction, services and extracurricular activities tailor-made to fit individual student.
